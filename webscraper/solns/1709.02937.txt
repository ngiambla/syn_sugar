let $\xi_0,\xi_1,\ldots$ be i.i.d. random variables with zero mean and unitvariance. consider a random taylor series of the form $f(z)=\sum_{k=0}^\infty\xi_k c_k z^k$, where $c_0,c_1,\ldots$ is a real sequence such that $c_n^2$ isregularly varying with index $\gamma-1$, where $\gamma>0$. we prove that$\mathbb{e} n[0,1-\epsilon] \sim \frac{\sqrt{\gamma}}{2\pi} |\log \epsilon|$ as$\epsilon \downarrow 0$, where $n[0,r]$ denotes the number of real zeroes of$f$ in the interval $[0,r]$.
