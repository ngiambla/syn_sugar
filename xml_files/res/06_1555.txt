 The applicant claimed in his Protection Visa application and before the Tribunal that he had a well-founded fear of persecution from the authorities in China because he is a Christian. 5 In a decision handed down on 6 April 2006, the Tribunal affirmed the decision of the delegate of the Minister.
 The reasons for the Tribunal's decision are summarised in this paragraph of the Tribunal's reasons: 'In the light of the information before it, the Tribunal accepts that the Applicant is a Christian and that he was brought up as a member of the officially-sanctioned Three Self Patriotic Movement in China.
 Further, while the Tribunal has to give reasons for its finding, there is no obligation in a Tribunal to give reasons for its reasons. 14 The reasons for the Tribunal's conclusions were based on its findings as to the believability of the applicant's claims.
