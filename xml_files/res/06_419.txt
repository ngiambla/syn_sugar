 He submitted brief written contentions of fact and law in which he argued that there was a nexus between the harm he feared and his political association.
 In particular, he submitted that the money lenders who were pursuing him were connected with political and criminal groups and that he would not be at risk if he were part of those groups.
 The RRT and, thus, his Honour accepted that there was a real chance that the appellant would suffer serious harm if he returned to his home town and was unable to repay his debt.
