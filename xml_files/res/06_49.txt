 The notice of appeal filed in these proceedings identified one ground, namely that the Magistrate erred in law in holding that the Tribunal did not err in failing to consider whether she had a well-founded fear of persecution for the reason of her political opinion.
 It is not apparent that the Tribunal fell into jurisdictional error, nor is it apparent that the Federal Magistrate erred in concluding that the Tribunal had not fallen into jurisdictional error. 6 The appellant's failure before the Tribunal arose primarily because of the non-acceptance by the Tribunal of her account of her experiences in Mongolia.
