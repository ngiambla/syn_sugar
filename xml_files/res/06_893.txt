 The appellant also supplied the Tribunal with a faxed copy of an affidavit from the head of his village, as well as from his father, stating that he had some criminal cases in the law courts pending against him and that the police had unlawfully detained his father and he for some days.
 The appellant also gave the Tribunal a letter from an advocate. 4 The appellant also claimed that he had medical problems with his eye as a result of the abuse he had suffered in India.
 The Tribunal accepted that the appellant had, on at least one occasion, been in police custody but it did not find that this had been in connection with alleged militant activity or any other Convention reason.
 The appellant did not dispute that he had authorised the Tribunal to speak to his doctor and that he had been invited by the Tribunal to comment on the doctor's evidence.
