 The application for special leave to appeal, instituted on 1 June 2006, was deemed to have been abandoned on 29 June 2006 under the High Court Rules , but it was subsequently reinstated by order of Kirby J on 14 August 2006.
 Because the RRT decision was given on 20 October 2004, for the purposes of s 477, the applicant was taken to have been notified of the RRT decision only on 1 December 2005 when the amendment took effect: see s 1 and Sch 1 cl 42 and cl 17 of the Migration Litigation Reform Act 2005 .
 It does not appear that it was argued that s 476 defines the jurisdiction of the Federal Magistrates Court and that s 477, by imposing a time limit upon the manner of its exercise, may not simply be limiting the jurisdiction.
