 He submitted that, as a general rule, this Court will decline to permit further evidence to be led on appeal unless it is established that the evidence could not with reasonable diligence have been obtained before trial.
 In the end, he concluded that the evidence taken as a whole clearly established that, despite the execution of the declaration of trust, Mr Yazbek had not genuinely intended to hold the shares on trust for anyone.
 For example, evidence could not be received of a contemporaneous oral statement by a declarant that, despite the use of the term "declaration of trust" in a document then executed, no such trust was actually intended.
