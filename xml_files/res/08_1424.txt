 In so doing, the Tribunal necessarily accepted that Mr Bryant suffered from a "disease" for the purposes of the SRC Act , i.e. materially, an ailment suffered by him that was contributed to in a material degree by his employment by the Commonwealth (see; the then definition of "disease" in s 4 of the SRC Act ).
 At the relevant time, the SRC Act provided that an "injury" included a "disease" (definition of "injury" in s 4 , SRC Act ). 39 Having reached the conclusion that Mr Bryant suffered from an "injury" for the purposes of the SRC Act , the task for the Tribunal was that laid down by s 24(5) of the SRC Act , to determine the degree of permanent impairment of the employee resulting from that injury under the provisions of the Approved Guide.
