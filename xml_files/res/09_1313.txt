 Mr Orford then said that he would like to put to Mr Heyward that, by not cross-examining the deponents, he would be accepting their evidence and accepting their statements.
 Mr Orford said that he wanted to be sure that Mr Heyward was appreciative of what the effect of his decision not to cross-examine would be.
 The Tribunal reiterated that, if Mr Heyward chose not to cross-examine, it would mean that the evidence was unchallenged and that it could be accepted.
