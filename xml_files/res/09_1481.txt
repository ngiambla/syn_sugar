 Article 21 of the Model Law empowers the Court, upon recognition of a foreign proceeding, whether main or non-main, where necessary to protect the assets of the debtor or the interests of the creditors to grant any appropriate relief including the forms of relief set out in paras (a) to (g) "at the request of the foreign representative".
 The order made in the UK Proceeding appointing the plaintiffs recited that the High Court of Justice made a decision and gave judgment to open insolvency proceedings in England and Wales by way of administration under the Insolvency Act 1986 (UK) (Insolvency Act) Schedule B1 in respect of the defendant, and that a reason for the decision was that the defendant was or was likely to become unable to pay its debts.
 Paragraphs (a), (b) and (c) of Order 4 made on 30 November 2009 reflect ss 440B (charge unenforceable), 440BA (liens and pledges) and 440C (owner or lessor unable to recover property used by company) of the Corporations Act .
