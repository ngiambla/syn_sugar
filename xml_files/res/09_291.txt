 The test stated in Johnson refers to possibilities, not probabilities, although the possibilities must be real and not remote: Ebner v Official Trustee in Bankruptcy [2000] HCA 63 ; (2000) 205 CLR 337 at 345 [7] per Gleeson CJ, McHugh, Gummow and Hayne JJ.
