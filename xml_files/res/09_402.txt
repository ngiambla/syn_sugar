 The Commissioner contends that the legislative intent is that it is the substance of the activities which must also amount to the carrying on of a business or adventure in the nature of trade.
 In my view, the subsequent question whether 'the acquisition relates to making supplies that would be input taxed' is a question of law which properly invokes the jurisdiction of this Court under s 44(1) of the AAT Act.
 The Commissioner submits that there is no evidence to support the finding that Swansea was acquiring a collection of 'sound saleable artwork' in order to 'turn it to account profitably' as 'part of carrying on an enterprise'.
 The Commissioner has also expressed the view (in Good and Services Tax Determination GST 2000/8 at [7]) that: The words "in the form of" have the effect of extending the meaning of enterprise beyond entities carrying on a business.
